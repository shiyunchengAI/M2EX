The Dallas Mavericks (16 - 13) won an overtime thriller Wednesday, 119 - 118, against the host Brooklyn Nets (8 - 21) on an historic night for Dirk Nowitzki. Nowitzki passed Shaquille O'Neal to move into sixth place on the NBA's career scoring list, scoring 22 points for 28,609 in his career. But it was the final two that were most important. With 19 seconds remaining in overtime and trailing 118 - 117, Nowitzki hit a layup to provide the Mavericks with the winning margin. The Mavericks held a 108 - 105 lead with six seconds remaining in regulation when Thaddeus Young drained a 25 - foot three - pointer to tie it. Young then blocked J.J. Barea's final attempt to force overtime. Young finished with a season - high 29 points, while Barea scored a career - high 32 points to lead the Mavericks. He added a season - high 11 assists.
