This was one of the tightest games of the season, as there were 17 ties and 15 lead changes in total, with neither team leading by more than eight points. Shooting was key, as Atlanta went 52 percent from the field, while Toronto hit just 40 percent from the field and 16 percent from three - point range. Defensive rebounding was a huge factor as well, with Atlanta winning that battle, 37 - 30. DeMar DeRozan went just 12 - of - 30 from the field, but he led the team with 28 points, eight rebounds and six assists. Serge Ibaka totaled 18 points and three rebounds. Cory Joseph started yet again and contributed 15 points, eight assists and four steals. Dennis Schroeder led the way for Atlanta, as he collected 26 points, six rebounds and five assists. Tim Hardaway Jr. started and tallied 20 points, six assists and two steals. Paul Millsap provided 21 points, six rebounds and four assists.
